Patient Metrics Guide & Glossary

Click on any medical parameter below to learn what it means, why it matters, and how it is measured:

Resting ECG Results

Measures electrical activity in your heart while you are resting. This can show signs of heart strain, thickening, or damage:

  • Normal: Healthy resting heart rhythm with standard electrical pathways.
  • ST-T Wave Abnormality: Minor deviations in the electrical waves. Can indicate minor oxygen restriction, fatigue, stress, or minor blockages.
  • Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH): Enlargement/thickening of the muscle walls of the main chamber. Commonly due to long-term high blood pressure.
ST Segment Slope during Exercise

The ST segment represents the interval when the heart ventricles are contracting. During exercise testing, its slope is a key indicator of cardiac workload health:

  • Upsloping (Normal): The standard healthy response where the line rises quickly as exercise ends, indicating high heart recovery efficiency.
  • Flat Line (Borderline): The line remains horizontal under stress, indicating possible mild strain or restricted oxygen supply.
  • Downsloping (Abnormal): The line drops below baseline under stress, suggesting significant blood flow restriction to the heart muscle.
Chest Pain Types Explained

Chest pain is categorized into four distinct categories based on its clinical characteristics:

  • Typical Angina (TA): Severe squeezing chest tightness or pressure, triggered by physical exertion or stress. Direct signal of temporary cardiac oxygen shortage.
  • Atypical Angina (ATA): Minor chest pain or discomfort that does not fit the typical description. Often not related to direct coronary artery disease.
  • Non-Anginal Pain (NAP): Sharp or stabbing pain that is usually skeletal, muscular, or respiratory (e.g. pain with deep breaths).
  • Asymptomatic (ASY): No chest pain or tightness whatsoever.
Blood Pressure, Cholesterol & Heart Rate
Resting Blood Pressure (mmHg): Systolic pressure in the arteries. Optimal is <120 mmHg. Chronic elevated pressure (over 130-140) forces the heart to work harder.
Serum Cholesterol (mg/dl): Fat levels in the blood. Desirable levels are <200 mg/dl. High cholesterol can contribute to arterial plaque.
Max Exercise Heart Rate (bpm): The peak heart rate achieved during effort. An efficient heart is capable of achieving a higher maximum heart rate. Recommended peak rate is estimated as 220 - Age.
Oldpeak (ST Depression): Measured as the vertical distance of ECG depression during exercise compared to rest. Larger values suggest higher stress/fatigue in heart muscle.
